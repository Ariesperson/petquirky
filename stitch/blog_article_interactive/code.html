Best UVB Lights for Leopard Geckos in 2026

By PetQuirky March 15, 2026 5 min read

Why UVB Matters

While once debated, 2026's leading herpetological research confirms that UVB lighting is not just a luxury—it's essential for the physiological well-being of leopard geckos. Even though they are crepuscular, providing a low-level UVB Ferguson Zone 1 environment mimics their natural exposure in the wild, aiding in Vitamin D3 synthesis and calcium metabolism.

Without proper lighting, these reptiles are prone to Metabolic Bone Disease (MBD), which can lead to permanent skeletal deformities. Modern technology has moved us away from generic bulbs to sophisticated spectrum-targeted solutions.

Positioning for Optimal Health

It is not just about the light, but where you place it. Ensure the UVB source is situated on the same side as the heat lamp to create a natural "sun patch" gradient. A distance of 10-12 inches (25-30cm) is recommended for most 5% T5 bulbs to ensure the UVI (UV Index) stays within the safe range of 0.5 to 1.5.
